Before you can register a PAX terminal in Taliup HQ, the terminal must exist in **PaxStore** — this is where each device is created, activated, and assigned its **TID** (terminal ID). The TID is required when adding devices to Taliup HQ.

## Before you start

- Access to **PaxStore** with the correct **Administrator Center** and **Reseller** account (if applicable).
- All terminals powered on and connected to the internet while you work in PaxStore.
- The acquirer **VAR sheet** (terminal ID, merchant ID, host settings, etc.) for the BroadPOS payment app.

## 1. Create terminals in PaxStore

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to PaxStore">
    Log in to PaxStore with your administrator credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm your Administrator Center">
    Confirm you are in the correct **Administrator Center** (administrators only).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm your Reseller account">
    If applicable, confirm you are in the correct **Reseller** account (administrators only).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Terminal Management">
    Navigate to **Terminal Management**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create and activate the Merchant">
    Create and **Activate** the Merchant in PaxStore if it does not already exist.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create and activate terminals">
    Under the Merchant, create and **Activate** the terminals for the device type:

    | Device | Terminals to create |
    |---|---|
    | **PAX Elys** | Two terminals: **L1400** (cashier) and **A3700** (customer display) |
    | **PAX A920 Pro** | One terminal: the **A920 Pro** |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Note the TID for each terminal">
    After activating each terminal, note its **TID**. You will enter these TIDs in Taliup HQ when registering devices.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm marketplace subscriptions">
    Before pushing apps, confirm the Reseller or Merchant is **subscribed** to the required apps in the PaxStore marketplace.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## 2. PAX Elys — push apps

The PAX Elys requires apps on both the **L1400** (cashier terminal) and the **A3700** (customer display).

### L1400 — Taliup Elys

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the L1400 terminal">
    In Terminal Management, open the **L1400** terminal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Push Task">
    Click the **Push Task** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Push Taliup Elys">
    Click **+ PUSH APP**, search for and select **Taliup Elys**, then click **OK** and **Activate**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### A3700 — three apps, in order

<Warning>
  Install apps on the A3700 in this exact order. **BroadPOS Manager** must be installed before the acquirer payment app. Install **Taliup Companion** last. Installing out of order can cause the card prompt to fail.
</Warning>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Push BroadPOS Manager">
    Terminal Management → open the **A3700** → **Push Task** → **+ PUSH APP** → search **BroadPOS Manager** → **OK** → **Activate**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Push the acquirer BroadPOS app">
    **Push Task** → **+ PUSH APP** → select the BroadPOS app for your acquirer (for example, BroadPOS-Elavon or BroadPOS-Nuvei) → enter the **Parameters** from the VAR sheet → **OK** → **Activate**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Push Taliup Companion">
    **Push Task** → **+ PUSH APP** → search **Taliup Companion** → **OK** → **Activate**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm all push tasks succeed">
    Wait for all Push Tasks to show **Success** before continuing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | What to try |
|---|---|
| PaxStore push never completes | Confirm the terminal is online in PaxStore, check Push Task status, verify the app is subscribed in the marketplace, then reboot the terminal and retry. |
| No card prompt after Companion accepts the order (PAX Elys) | BroadPOS is missing or misconfigured on the A3700. Re-push **BroadPOS Manager**, then the acquirer BroadPOS app with VAR parameters, then **Taliup Companion** — in that order. |
| No card prompt on A920 Pro | BroadPOS is missing or misconfigured. Re-push **BroadPOS Manager**, then the acquirer BroadPOS app with VAR parameters, then **Taliup POS** — in that order. |
